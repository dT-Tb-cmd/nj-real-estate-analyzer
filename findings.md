## Q2 — Price-per-sqft trend (2012-2026)

All four Mercer County ZIPs grew at remarkably similar rates from 2012
to 2026 — **4.50% to 4.67% compound annual growth**. The spread is only
0.17 percentage points, which tells us **the region moves as one housing
market**. Our target, **Lawrenceville (08648), actually led the pack**
at 4.67% CAGR, rising from $147/sqft to $279/sqft (+89.4%). Hamilton
grew slowest at 4.50% (still nearly doubled). Implication: buying in
Lawrenceville means a coherent regional market with steady appreciation,
not a bubble. Differentiation between ZIPs will come from schools,
commute, and tax burden — not from raw price trends.

## Q4 — Tax burden comparison

On a hypothetical $500K home: Hamilton costs **$13,600/yr** in property
tax, Lawrenceville **$11,400/yr**, Pennington **≥$9,150**, Princeton Jct
**≥$7,000** (last two are lower bounds — Census top-codes above $10K).
**Hamilton is $2,200/yr more expensive than Lawrenceville** on the same
home — $66K over a 30-year mortgage. For our budget, **Lawrenceville
has a clear tax advantage over Hamilton** and is roughly break-even with
the premium ZIPs once their higher absolute home values are factored in.
